Why European Aesthetics

The European Standard
in Aesthetic Medicine

In Europe, aesthetic procedures follow stricter clinical regulations and a deeper respect for facial anatomy. Neurotoxins such as Botox are treated as precise therapeutic tools — not mass-market cosmetic products. Many clinics prioritize revenue over quality, which can lead to over-diluted neurotoxins, artificial results, and shorter-lasting treatments.

Post-Treatment Care

Post Treatment Instructions

01

Avoid Touching Injection Sites

Do not massage, rub, or apply pressure to treated areas for at least 24 hours after the procedure.

02

Avoid Physical Activity

Avoid strenuous exercise or heavy lifting for 24 hours after treatment.

03

Avoid Heat Exposure

Avoid saunas, tanning beds, hot tubs, and excessive sun exposure for two weeks after treatment.

04

Avoid Alcohol

Do not consume alcohol for 24 hours after the procedure to minimize swelling and bruising.

05

No Makeup for 24 Hours

Avoid applying makeup for the first 24 hours to reduce risk of irritation and infection.

06

Skin Moisturization

Use gentle, fragrance-free moisturizers to support skin healing. Avoid products containing retinoids or AHA/BHA acids.

07

Arnica Ointment

Apply arnica ointment to reduce bruising or swelling if necessary following your treatment.

08

Follow-Up Appointment

A follow-up visit is recommended two weeks after treatment. If severe symptoms occur such as swelling, headaches, or vision problems, contact your provider immediately.
